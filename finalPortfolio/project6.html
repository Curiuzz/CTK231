The Ladder

The Ladder
By: Christopher Posner

What would younger me say
If I told him I got saved
I got saved from the past
I got saved from the pain

What would younger me say
If I told him I haven’t changed
I’m still stuck in the past
I’m still haunted by the pain

When you spend 5 years trying
To cope with your scars
To cope with your wounds
To let down your guard
Is it crazy that a surgery can’t even help?

When you spend 5 years dying
Falling so far down a hole
As far down as the pain in your nerves
Is it crazy that a ladder can’t even help?

If I told you how it feels
To resist the tears forming in your eyes
Hoping for something to ease the pain
Would you sympathize?

If I told you how it feels
To stand alone with your broken spine
And hope the pain wouldn’t flare up
Would you stand by my side?

If I woke up
And my skin was pale and green
And I walked with both hands
Stretched out in front of me
Would I be any different?

What would future me say
If I told him I wanted to stay this way
Would he beg me to wake up?
Would he beg me to change?

I was handed the ladder
When I was so far down
How do you expect me to climb up now?

But I was handed the ladder
When I needed it most
So why am I still here?

I was handed the ladder
When younger me was fighting
And for that poor soul
Step by step, I’m climbing
