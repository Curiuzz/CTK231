Stray Kitten
By: Christopher Posner

I’m a witness of a crime
A witness of a burglary
A witness of a woman
Who witnessed herself steal her own heart

She was a beautiful being
More beautiful than most
With eyes that shined and sparkled
Bright like the starlight at night

She brought with herself a fragrance
That opened up a new world
She put on 50 bottles of perfume
Nobody knew her natural scent
Only the flowery one that was carried with her
So she could hide herself better

She broke the mirror in her life
Her glasses cracked in half
But mine didn’t

If only 1 of 100 say something hurtful
She listens to the one
The smallest things would bug her
Like she had OCD
But only when she thought about herself

As she lost touch with her outside
She lost touch with her inside too
Every day
Bit by bit
From her stomach to her trash
She lost her hunger
She lost herself too

Her book was left shut
Nobody could add to it
Only her own dark thoughts
Were added to the pages

Her world went from day to night
Just during the day
Her mind, the fluctuating stock market
Not knowing when to buy or sell

So she bought the bad opinions
Mostly from herself
And took her life away
Beauty, unknown yet clear
Gone with her

I wish she knew
I wished she would look at herself different
I’m not religious, but I prayed
I prayed she wouldn’t give up
I prayed she would stay

Cute as a puppy
Pretty as a butterfly
But she was misplaced
She was in the lost and found
As I look back
It’s clear to me now
She was a stray kitten
Beautiful and lost

I’m a witness of a crime
A crime a girl committed on herself
If only she knew that 5 extra pounds
Didn’t stop her from being the most beautiful person in the room
